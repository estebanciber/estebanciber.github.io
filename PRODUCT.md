# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Potential employers, creative directors, and collaborators reviewing María José Arévalo's work. They land on the portfolio from LinkedIn, a job application, or word of mouth. They are looking for evidence of craft — not a résumé in HTML, but a visual statement of skill across photography, graphic design, and video production. They scan quickly for range, taste, and professionalism.

## Product Purpose

Showcase María José's creative work to open doors for freelance, agency, or in-house opportunities. Success means a visitor finishes the page with a clear sense of her visual voice and wants to reach out. It is a portfolio first, not a blog or a social feed.

## Brand Personality

Editorial, warm, composed. The tone is confident without being loud — the work speaks, the page frames it. This is a student portfolio that does not read as student work.

Three-word personality: **editorial, warm, composed**.

## Anti-references

- **Generic portfolio templates**: card grids with uniform thumbnails, "About Me" with a centered headshot, identical project pages. No template scaffolding.
- **SaaS or agency vibe**: gradient CTAs, floating navs, testimonials carousel, "Let's work together" hero sections. This is a portfolio, not a pitch deck.
- **Over-designed for its scale**: a single-page portfolio with too many layout moves, competing type systems, or decorative flourishes. The work is the focus.
- **"Professional" that reads as cold**: sterile white backgrounds, cramped sans-serif body, no warmth or materiality. A creative portfolio can be serious without being corporate.

## Design Principles

1. **The work leads.** Every layout decision exists to frame photography, design, or video. If a section calls attention to itself instead of the content, it's wrong.
2. **Editorial pacing, not template sections.** Each section has its own rhythm — hero, profile, gallery, quote, video, contact. No two sections share the same grid. Variety is the proof of range.
3. **Typography is the voice.** The serif/sans pair carries the editorial identity. The type choices earn their elegance through proportion and contrast, not decoration.
4. **Photography is the texture.** Grayscale portraits warm to color on hover, captions fade in, spreads breathe. The images are not thumbnails in a grid; they're editorial frames.
5. **Motion that follows the eye.** Scroll reveals are paced for reading, not for spectacle. Intentional sequence, not staggered everything.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA baseline
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on every animation (already implemented)
- All interactive elements keyboard-navigable with visible focus rings (already has `focus-ring` mixin)
- Semantic HTML with ARIA where needed (already has `aria-labels` on navs and sections)
- Spanish-first copy with clear, scannable section headers
